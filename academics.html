Our Approach

Our Curriculum

At Eastgate Academy, we follow a balanced curriculum that meets national standards while incorporating innovative teaching methods to engage young learners at every stage of their development.

Our programme is designed to develop critical thinking, creativity, and a lifelong love of learning — equipping every child with the skills they need for the future.

National curriculum aligned
Continuous assessment model
Small class sizes for personal attention
Bilingual support available
Grade Levels

Learning at Every Stage

Tailored programmes designed to meet each child exactly where they are.

Kindergarten (Ages 5–6)

Our kindergarten programme focuses on developing foundational skills through play-based learning and hands-on activities in a warm, nurturing environment.

Key Focus Areas

  • Phonics and early reading skills
  • Number recognition and basic math concepts
  • Motor skill development
  • Social-emotional learning
  • Creative expression through art and music
Ages 5–6

Lower Primary (P1–P3)

We build on core academic skills while nurturing curiosity, collaboration and a genuine love for learning in a structured but engaging classroom environment.

Core Subjects

  • English Language & Literacy
  • Mathematics
  • Science & Environment
  • Social Studies
  • ICT and Creative Arts
P1 – P3

Upper Primary (P4–P6)

Our upper primary programme prepares learners for Junior High through deeper subject mastery, critical thinking and project-based learning experiences.

Subjects & Enrichment

  • Advanced English & Literature
  • Mathematics & Pre-Algebra
  • Integrated Science
  • History & Government
  • Reading Programmes & Cultural Projects
P4 – P6
How We Measure Progress

Assessment & Reporting

We believe in continuous, fair assessment to monitor student growth and provide targeted support where it's needed most.

Formative Assessments

Quizzes, classwork and teacher observations help us track learning in real time and adjust our teaching accordingly.

Summative Assessments

End-of-term exams give a comprehensive evaluation of each student's overall subject understanding and mastery.

Project Work

Hands-on projects and presentations build research, communication and teamwork skills beyond the classroom.

Parents receive report cards three times a year and are warmly invited to attend progress conferences with class teachers.
